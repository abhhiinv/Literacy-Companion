# Project Design Context: Literacy Companion

## Design Context

### Users
Adult learners, including the elderly, who are improving their literacy skills. They may have varying levels of technological comfort and potentially age-related visual or cognitive needs. Their goal is to gain confidence and functional literacy in a dignified, supportive environment.

### Brand Personality
**Patient, Clear, Empowering.**
The interface should feel like a supportive mentor—reliable, easy to understand, and encouraging without being patronizing.

### Aesthetic Direction
High-accessibility modernism. This means high-contrast palettes, generous whitespace, and a focus on "dignified" adult education. It should **NOT** look like a children's learning app. It should feel like a premium, professional tool that is exceptionally easy to use.

### Design Principles
1.  **Extreme Legibility**: Use large font sizes (baseline 18-20px), high-contrast color pairings (targeting WCAG AAA where possible), and generous line-height for readability.
2.  **Affirmative Affordances**: Buttons and interactive elements must look unmistakably clickable with large touch targets (min 48x48px) and clear state changes.
3.  **Radial Simplicity**: Navigation should be linear and obvious. Minimize nested menus or complex gestures. Every screen should have one primary clear action.
4.  **Dignified Tone**: Use mature color palettes (tinted neutrals, deep blues/greens) and professional iconography to ensure adult learners feel respected.
5.  **Feedback-Rich**: Provide immediate, clear visual and auditory feedback for every action (e.g., word highlights, success animations).

## Quality Bar
**Flagship Hackathon Entry**: Every interaction must be intentional, smooth, and bug-free to stand out in a competitive Google hackathon environment.
